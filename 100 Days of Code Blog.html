The Journey to Coding

Introduction

Most people never get past their first 100 days of learning to code. I'm trying!

How This Works

This blog that charts my first steps, links I liked, and how I felt. When you feel stuck, I hope it helps you move along more quickly

Who I am

In 2020, I joined Hack Club, a nonprofit that helps high schoolers start after-school computer clubs. My job was Chief Operating Officer, which means I work directly with the founder. When hired, I was the only non-technical person on the team. In fact, I didn't even know what HTML was. Often, in working with others, I couldn't understand what they were saying.

Background

Growing up in the 1980s, no one had computers or the internet. Here I am on the right. It's my senior year in high school in the 1990s, and I've never even seen a computer yet:

Today, fewer than .5% of the human population can code. Fewer than half of schools even try to teach any tech.

My Goals

  1. Deeply understand computers and the internet
  2. Chronicle what it helps me do
  3. Share so others can do the same.

Can I do it? Let's go!
